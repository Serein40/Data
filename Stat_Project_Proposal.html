Questions of Interest

Our project focuses on exploring various soccer statistics to gain insights into trends and factors that may influence team success in the league. Specifically, we will focus on the following questions:

  • What patterns emerge from soccer player statistics across the league?
    • We will analyze individual player performance data, such as goals, assists, and minutes played, to identify trends and anomalies. By grouping these stats by player positions and teams, we aim to uncover patterns that contribute to overall team success.
  • How does goal differential impact a team’s final standing in the league?
    • This question addresses one of the most critical metrics in soccer: goal differential, which is the difference between the goals a team scores and the goals they concede. We’ll investigate whether a higher goal differential correlates with a better final position in the league standings.
  • Can we predict the ranking of a team based on its goal differential?
    • Using statistical analysis, we will explore whether goal differential can serve as a reliable predictor of team rankings, providing insights into how crucial this metric is to a team’s overall success.

Dataset(s)

For our analysis, we will use the following publicly available datasets that cover player and team statistics from the England Premier League for the 2018-2019 season:

  1. ESPN Soccer Data
    • This dataset provides a comprehensive collection of player performance data, including individual goals, assists, and other important metrics that are vital for player-level analysis.
  2. FootyStats: Stats CSV
    • This dataset contains match-level data for the Premier League, including team statistics such as goals scored, goals conceded, and other performance-related metrics. It will allow us to perform team-level analysis.
  3. England Premier League Players 2018-2019 Stats
    • A CSV file with detailed information about each player’s performance throughout the season. This dataset will be used to examine individual player statistics and trends across various positions.
  4. England Premier League Teams 2018-2019 Stats
    • A dataset that tracks team performance metrics across the season, which will be useful for our analysis of team statistics and goal differential.

By combining these datasets, we will be able to assess both individual player performance and team performance, including the relationship between team success and goal differential.

Description of Data

Player Stats

This dataset includes detailed performance data for each player in the Premier League during the 2018-2019 season. Key columns include: - Goals Scored: The number of goals a player scored during the season. - Assists: The number of assists a player made. - Minutes Played: The total number of minutes a player participated in the league. - Shots on Target: The number of shots that were on target during the season.

By examining these columns, we will identify which players performed at the highest level and how their individual stats contribute to their team’s success.

Team Stats

This dataset provides an overview of team performance during the same season. Key columns include: - Goals Scored: The total number of goals a team scored during the season. - Goals Allowed: The total number of goals a team allowed during the season. - Goal Differential: The difference between goals scored and goals conceded (calculated as Goals Scored - Goals Allowed). - Final Position: The team’s final position in the league standings.

Goal differential is a critical statistic, as it often plays a role in determining a team’s ranking when two or more teams have the same number of points. This will be central to our analysis.

Description of Graph

The main graphical representation of our data will be a scatter plot that visually shows the relationship between: - Goal Differential (X-axis), which is calculated as goals scored - goals conceded, and - League Position (Y-axis), representing the team’s final standing in the Premier League after all matches.

In the scatter plot: - Each point will represent a team, with the x-coordinate corresponding to their final position in the league, and the y-coordinate showing their goal differential. - This plot will help us visually identify whether teams with a higher goal differential tend to secure better league positions. Additionally, we will explore if there are any outliers or unexpected patterns.

Type of Inference Method

We will use simple linear regression to model the relationship between goal differential and team ranking in the league. Linear regression will help us quantify how much a team’s goal differential affects its final standing.

By applying linear regression: - The independent variable will be goal differential (goals scored - goals conceded). - The dependent variable will be the league position (rank).

This statistical method will allow us to estimate the strength of the relationship between these two variables and make predictions about team rankings based on their goal differential.
